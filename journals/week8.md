# HugeTiny1616 Case
I'm making a case for my devboard.

## Monday, 25. May
Since I've already worked with Fusion 360 everything went pretty smooth, first I imported the 3d model of my devboard, then I downloaded a 3d model for the buttons(since kicad doesn't export them for me) and placed them on the correct position, which took way longer than it should have(since I only use them for checking the height). Then I created a sketch from the bottom of the board with walls and small pillars to let it "float" in the middle. After that I extruded it and added a sketch on the fron to make a hole for the usb-c port. After that I tried to fix the holes in the bottom that the pins somehow(don't ask me how) created, but I gave up on it after some time.

**Time spend: 30min**

## Friday, 29. May
Removedf the holes by editing the sketch that was created from the bottom of the devboard, I still don't know why they're there, but it worked in the end. For some reason Fusion360 didn't really show what holes I deleted in the sketch, but with a bit of trial and error it worked.

**Time spend: 10min**

## Total time spend: 40min
